---
title: "Logging Supervision Hours"
description: "Record supervision sessions and track hours toward ACBC certification."
---

Soul Care Manager includes a supervision log for counselors working through ACBC certification or fellowship. Log entries are tied to individual counselee cases, giving you a clear record of which cases were supervised and when.

## Add a supervision log entry

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the counselee's record">
    Click the counselee's name from your list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Supervision tab">
    Find the Supervision tab on the counselee's record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Entry">
    Fill in the supervision details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The entry is saved to the counselee's record and counted toward your supervision totals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Log entry fields

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Date** | The date of the supervision session |
| **Supervisor name** | The name of the supervisor you met with |
| **Hours** | Time spent in supervision for this case, in decimal format (e.g. 1.5 for 90 minutes) |
| **Topics covered** | Select from common supervision topics or add your own |
| **Scripture references** | Any passages discussed during supervision |
| **Notes** | Free-text notes about what was covered |

## Who can see supervision logs

- **Counselors** see their own supervision entries across all counselee records
- **Center admins and supervisors** can see supervision entries across all counselors in the center

Supervision logs are not visible to counselees — they are internal records only.

## Supervision log vs. session notes

Supervision logs and session notes serve different purposes and are stored separately:

| | Supervision Log | Session Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Document what was covered in supervision | Document what happened in a counseling session |
| **Who it's about** | The supervision meeting with your supervisor | The counseling session with your counselee |
| **Where it lives** | Supervision tab on the counselee's record | Session View and Sessions list |

## Recording session type

When logging a session for ACBC tracking purposes, you can note whether it was recorded (audio or video) and attach the recording as a file. This is relevant for counselors in the initial certification phase where recorded sessions may be reviewed by a supervisor.
