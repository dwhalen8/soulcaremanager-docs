---
title: "Appointment Reminders"
description: "Automatic email reminders sent to counselees before their sessions."
---

Soul Care Manager can automatically send counselees an email reminder before each scheduled appointment. Reminders are sent based on each counselor's configured lead time.

## How reminders work

When an appointment is coming up, the system checks whether a reminder should go out based on the counselor's **reminder hours** setting. If the appointment falls within that window and a reminder hasn't been sent yet, it goes out automatically.

Reminders include:
- The appointment date and time (in the counselee's timezone)
- The session type and location (if set)
- Your center's branding

## Configure your reminder timing

Each counselor sets how many hours before an appointment their reminders go out.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your profile">
    Click your name at the bottom of the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find Appointment Reminders">
    Look for the reminder hours setting in your notification preferences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your preferred lead time">
    Common choices are 24 hours or 48 hours before the appointment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    All future reminders will go out at the new timing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Turn off reminders for a specific counselee

If a counselee prefers not to receive reminders, you can disable them on a per-counselee basis.

Open the counselee's record, find the **Appointment Reminders** toggle in their profile, and turn it off. Reminders will no longer be sent for that person regardless of your counselor-level setting.

## Send a reminder manually

If you want to send a reminder outside the automatic schedule — for example, if a counselee seems likely to forget — you can trigger one manually from the appointment.

Open the appointment from your calendar and use the **Send Reminder** option. A reminder email goes out immediately.

<Note>
  Manual reminders can be sent even if automatic reminders are turned off for that counselee.
</Note>
