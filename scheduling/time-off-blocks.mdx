---
title: "Time-Off Blocks"
description: "Block out vacations, conferences, and other extended unavailability."
---

Time-off blocks let you mark periods when you're not available for sessions. They're respected by the public scheduling page — counselees won't be able to request appointments during a blocked period.

## Add a time-off block

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your Schedule">
    Click **Schedule** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Blockouts">
    Look for the **Time Off** or **Blockouts** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the date range">
    Choose a start and end date. For a single day off, set both to the same date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the time range (optional)">
    To block only part of a day — say, a morning conference — set a start and end time. Leave these blank to block the full day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The block appears on your calendar and is immediately reflected in your scheduling availability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recurring time-off

If you have a regularly recurring unavailability — for example, you're at a staff meeting every other Friday morning — you can set a time-off block to repeat on the same schedule.

Enable the recurrence option when creating the block and choose the frequency, just like a recurring appointment.

## Overlap warnings

If you try to schedule an appointment that overlaps with an existing time-off block, Soul Care Manager will warn you. You can proceed anyway if needed — the warning is informational, not a hard block.

## Time-off blocks vs. availability exceptions

| Use case | What to use |
|---|---|
| Vacation, conference, extended leave | **Time-off block** |
| One afternoon you're unavailable | **Time-off block** (partial day) |
| One day you're available earlier than usual | **Availability exception** (extra) |
| Removing a few hours from an otherwise available day | **Availability exception** (block) |

For short one-off adjustments to your schedule, availability exceptions (set in your availability settings) are usually the right tool. Time-off blocks are better for anything multi-day or recurring.
