---
title: "Setting Your Availability"
description: "Configure your working hours and let the system know when you're available to meet."
---

Soul Care Manager uses your availability settings to power the public self-scheduling page, where prospective counselees can request appointments during your open slots. Getting this set up correctly means you'll only receive requests during times you're actually available.

## Center-level vs. counselor-level availability

Availability works at two levels:

- **Center-level**: Your center administrator sets default working hours, days of the week, booking lead time, and booking window under Center Settings. These serve as the baseline for all counselors.
- **Counselor-level**: Individual counselors can define their own weekly availability schedule, which overrides the center defaults for their specific slots.

## Setting your weekly availability

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to your Schedule">
    Click **Schedule** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Availability settings">
    Look for the **Availability** or **My Availability** option.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your hours per day">
    For each day you're available, set a start and end time. Toggle off any days you don't take appointments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Your availability is now reflected on the public scheduling page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Availability exceptions

If you need to adjust your availability for a specific date — say, you're available earlier than usual one day or unavailable for part of an afternoon — you can add an availability exception for that date without affecting your regular schedule.

Exceptions come in two types:
- **Block**: removes a window of time from an otherwise available day
- **Extra**: adds a window of time on an otherwise unavailable day

## Time-off blocks

For longer periods of unavailability — vacations, conferences, extended leave — use time-off blocks rather than availability exceptions. See [Time-Off Blocks](/scheduling/time-off-blocks) for details.

## How the scheduler uses your availability

When a prospective counselee visits your scheduling page, the system calculates open slots by combining:

1. Your weekly availability
2. Any exceptions for the requested date
3. Your existing appointments (so slots already booked are hidden)
4. Your time-off blocks
5. The center's booking lead time and window settings

Only genuinely open slots are shown — the counselee can't request a time that's already taken or outside your available hours.
