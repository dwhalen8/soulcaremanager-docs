---
title: "The Counselee Portal"
description: "A separate, secure area where counselees log in to view their appointments, homework, and forms."
---

The counselee portal is a simplified, private interface your counselees use to interact with their counseling. It looks and works differently from the main counselor app by design — counselees only see what's relevant to them, nothing more.

## What counselees can do in the portal

| Section | What's there |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | A greeting, daily Scripture quote, next upcoming appointment, current homework, and recent forms |
| **Appointments** | Upcoming and past sessions with date, time, location, and session type |
| **Forms** | Pending forms to fill out and past submissions they can review |
| **Resources & Homework** | Assigned homework and reading material, with the ability to mark items complete |
| **Documents** | Files you've explicitly shared with them, available to download |
| **Prayer Requests** | A place to submit prayer requests directly to their counselor |

## What counselees cannot see

The portal is intentionally limited. Counselees cannot see:

- Your session notes or counselor documentation
- Other counselees' records
- Highlights or annotations you've made on their forms
- Any information about your center's internal operations

## Accessing the portal

Counselees access the portal at the same URL as the main app — [soulcaremanager.com](https://soulcaremanager.com) — but log in with their own counselee account. The portal interface loads automatically based on their role.

## Portal vs. main app

The portal and the counselor app are entirely separate interfaces. If you have a counselee role on your own account (for example, you're also receiving counseling), you can switch to the portal view using the role switcher in the sidebar.
