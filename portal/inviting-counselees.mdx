---
title: "Inviting Counselees to the Portal"
description: "Give a counselee access to their portal account so they can view appointments, homework, and forms."
---

Counselees don't have portal access by default. You invite them explicitly when you're ready to give them access — this keeps things simple for counselees who won't use the portal and avoids sending unnecessary account creation emails.

## Send a portal invitation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the counselee's record">
    Click their name from your counselee list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Portal Access section">
    Look for the portal or account section on their record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Invite to Portal">
    Enter their email address if it isn't already on file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    The counselee receives an email with a link to create their account and set a password. The link is valid for a limited time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the counselee does next

The counselee clicks the link in their email, creates a password, and verifies their email address. Once verified they can log in to the portal immediately.

<Note>
  If the counselee doesn't receive the invitation email, ask them to check their spam folder. You can resend the invite from their record if needed.
</Note>

## Counselee login

Counselees log in at [soulcaremanager.com/login](https://soulcaremanager.com/login) using the email address their invitation was sent to. The portal interface loads automatically — they won't see the counselor app.

## What happens to their record if they don't accept

If a counselee never accepts the invitation, their counselee record continues to exist and work normally on your end. The invitation only affects their portal access — everything else (sessions, notes, forms, files) is unaffected.

## Revoking portal access

If you need to remove a counselee's portal access, contact your center admin. Access can be revoked without affecting the counselee's record or history in the system.
