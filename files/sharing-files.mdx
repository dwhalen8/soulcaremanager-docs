---
title: "Sharing Files"
description: "Share files with counselees or other counselors, with a full audit log of every action."
---

Files in Soul Care Manager are private by default. Sharing is always an explicit action — nothing is visible to a counselee or another counselor unless you share it directly.

## Share a file with a counselee

When you share a file with a counselee, it appears in the **Documents** section of their portal for them to view and download.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the file">
    Navigate to the file from wherever it's attached — a session, note, or the counselee's record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Share">
    Open the file's sharing options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share with counselee">
    Select the option to share with the counselee. The file appears in their portal immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Share a file with another counselor

You can share a file with another counselor at your center — useful when collaborating on a case or seeking input.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the file">
    Navigate to the file from wherever it's attached.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Share">
    Open the file's sharing options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the counselor">
    Choose a counselor from your center. Add an optional note explaining the context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share">
    The counselor now has access to view and download the file.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Revoking access

To remove someone's access to a shared file:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the file's sharing settings">
    Find the file and open its share panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the active share">
    The panel lists everyone currently with access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke">
    Click **Revoke** next to the person whose access you want to remove. Access is removed immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Share audit log

Every share and revoke action is logged automatically. The audit log shows:

- Who shared the file
- Who it was shared with
- When the share was granted
- When access was revoked (if applicable)

This gives you a complete record of who has seen each file and when — important for maintaining appropriate confidentiality around counseling records.

<Note>
  The audit log is permanent and cannot be edited or deleted. This is by design — it ensures an accurate record of file access over the life of the counseling relationship.
</Note>
