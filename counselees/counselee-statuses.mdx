---
title: "Counselee Statuses"
description: "Understanding active, prospective, and archived counselees."
---

Every counselee in Soul Care Manager has a status that reflects where they are in the counseling relationship. Status affects how a counselee appears in your list and what actions are available on their record.

## The three statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active** | Currently in regular sessions |
| **Prospective** | Has reached out or submitted intake paperwork but hasn't started regular sessions yet |
| **Archived** | No longer receiving counseling; record is retained but removed from your active list |

## Prospective counselees

A counselee is created as prospective when they come in through the intake invite flow or the public scheduling page. Once you've had an initial meeting and decided to take them on, change their status to active.

Prospective counselees appear in your counselee list but are visually distinguished from active counselees so your working caseload stays clear.

## Archiving a counselee

When a counseling relationship ends, archive the counselee rather than deleting them. Archiving:

- Removes them from your active counselee list
- Preserves all their notes, sessions, forms, files, and history
- Allows you to reactivate them later if they return

To archive a counselee, open their record and use the **Archive** option. You'll be asked to confirm before the action takes effect.

<Note>
  Archiving is reversible. If a former counselee returns, you can restore their record and all prior history will still be there.
</Note>

## Reactivating a counselee

To restore an archived counselee, filter your counselee list to show archived records, open the record, and change their status back to active.

## A note on permanent deletion

Permanent deletion is available to center admins for duplicate or test records only. Unlike archiving, deletion cannot be undone — all associated notes, sessions, forms, and files are removed permanently. Use it with care.
