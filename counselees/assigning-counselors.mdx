---
title: "Assigning Counselors"
description: "How counselor assignments work for individual counselees."
---

Soul Care Manager is built for centers where more than one counselor sees counselees. Each counselee can have a primary counselor, a co-counselor, and additional counselors for joint sessions — and can be reassigned without losing any history.

## Primary counselor

Every counselee has one primary counselor. This is the person responsible for the counseling relationship. By default, the counselor who creates the counselee record is set as the primary counselor.

To change the primary counselor, open the counselee's record and edit the **Counselor** field in their profile.

## Co-counselor

A co-counselor can be added to a counselee's record when two counselors are working together on a case. Co-counselors have the same access to the counselee's record as the primary counselor.

## Additional counselors for joint sessions

When scheduling a joint session (for couples, families, or group meetings), you can include additional counselees and counselors on a single appointment. Each counselor sees their own notes tab within the shared session view.

## Who can see which counselees

Access to counselee records is scoped by role:

- **Counselors** can only see counselees assigned to them as primary counselor, co-counselor, or additional counselor
- **Center admins** can see all counselees in the center

This means a counselor cannot accidentally view another counselor's caseload, and reassigning a counselee changes whose list they appear on immediately.

## Reassigning a counselee

To reassign a counselee to a different primary counselor:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the counselee record">
    Click the counselee's name from your list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit their profile">
    Find the **Counselor** field and select the new primary counselor from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The counselee now appears on the new counselor's list. All prior notes, sessions, and history are preserved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Reassigning a counselee does not notify the new counselor automatically. Let them know directly so they can review the case history before meeting with the counselee.
</Note>
